# Southern Bro Enterprises Platform Comparison Report

## 1. Objective

Compare low-cost website platform options that Southern Bro Enterprises could use while systems are being restored, with special attention to ease of launch, lead capture, SEO, and integration with HubSpot.

## 2. Evaluation Criteria

- startup cost
- free tier or trial availability
- ease of editing
- SEO flexibility
- CRM and form integration
- design control
- reliability for a small business

## 3. Platform Comparison

| Platform | Approximate Entry Cost | Free Option | Strengths | Limitations | Best Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| HubSpot Content Hub | Free tools available; paid tiers vary | Yes | Strong CRM connection, forms, contact tracking, email follow-up, fast integration with current stack | Full CMS flexibility depends on tier and setup | Best overall if HubSpot stays the core system |
| WordPress | Free software; hosting cost varies; managed plans often start low monthly | Yes, depending on host or WordPress.com plan | Very strong SEO, blogging, plugins, flexible long-term growth | Needs maintenance and plugin discipline | Best long-term content and SEO option |
| Wix | Lower-cost paid plans available; free creation option | Yes | Fast setup, simple editing, easy for non-technical staff | Less flexible for advanced systems and CRM-heavy operations | Good for quick launch if simplicity is the top priority |
| Webflow | Starter free plan; paid publishing plans required for live production use | Yes | Polished design control, modern visual quality | Higher learning curve, paid publishing needed for serious use | Best for design-heavy marketing sites |
| GitHub Pages | Free | Yes | Good for temporary static landing pages, custom domain support, HTTPS | No built-in CRM, weak for non-technical editing, no native forms | Best temporary backup site or outage page |

## 4. Recommendation Summary

### Best Immediate Fit

`HubSpot + simple website rebuild`

Reason:

- the company already uses HubSpot
- lead capture and contact management can stay in one system
- it reduces duplicate tools during a recovery period
- management can track inquiries more easily

### Best Long-Term Growth Fit

`WordPress + HubSpot integration`

Reason:

- stronger long-term SEO and resource/blog flexibility
- easier expansion into service pages and content marketing
- can still connect to HubSpot forms and CRM

### Best Temporary Backup Site

`GitHub Pages`

Reason:

- free hosting
- useful if the main website is still down
- can publish a clean status page, contact info, and request links quickly

## 5. Platform Ranking for Southern Bro

1. HubSpot-first rebuild
2. WordPress with HubSpot integration
3. GitHub Pages as temporary backup presence
4. Wix for speed and simplicity
5. Webflow if visual polish becomes the top priority and budget grows

## 6. Suggested Decision

### Option A: Fast Recovery

- rebuild the public site with the easiest platform available now
- connect forms directly to HubSpot
- launch Home, About, Services, Request Quote, and Contact first

### Option B: Long-Term Growth

- use WordPress for main content and SEO
- keep HubSpot as CRM and lead engine
- add blog, service pages, and scalable content strategy

## 7. Platform Research Notes

As of March 13, 2026:

- WordPress.com lists a free plan and paid plans starting at low monthly pricing when billed annually.
- Wix lists free site creation and paid plans starting at a low monthly price point for custom-domain business use.
- Webflow lists a Starter free option, with paid site plans for production publishing.
- GitHub Pages documentation supports free static hosting, HTTPS, and custom domains.
- HubSpot provides free CRM and form tools, which is valuable because the company already uses HubSpot.
